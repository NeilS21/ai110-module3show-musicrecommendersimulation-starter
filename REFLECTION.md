# Evaluation Reflection: Profile Comparisons

## Why Gym Hero Keeps Showing Up

**High-Energy Pop vs Conflicting Energy-Mood:**
High-Energy Pop wants pop + happy + 0.9 energy. Gym Hero ranks #2 at 69.4 because: pop genre (+40) + high energy match (+19.4) + acoustic (+10) = solid score, even though it's "intense" mood, not "happy."

Conflicting Energy-Mood wants pop + sad + 0.95 energy. Gym Hero ranks #1 at 69.6 because the same factors apply, but now there's no mood match (sad ≠ intense), so Gym Hero beats Sunrise City which is pop + happy (mood mismatch is the same problem).

**Why This Makes Sense:** Gym Hero is a generic "energetic pop" song. It scores well when energy is the priority and the user's genre/energy align. It loses when mood matters more.

---

## Acoustic vs Electronic Divide

**Chill Lofi vs Acoustic+Electronic Paradox:**
Chill Lofi (lofi + happy + 0.1 energy + wants acoustic) gets Library Rain as #1 (lofi genre + low energy + acoustic).

Acoustic+Electronic Paradox (electronic + happy + 0.8 energy + wants acoustic) gets Neon Pulse as #1 (electronic genre + high energy, but NOT acoustic at 0.12).

**Why This Matters:** Both users wanted acoustic, but they got completely different results. Chill Lofi got exactly what they asked for. Acoustic+Electronic couldn't—because all the electronic songs are electric, and all the acoustic songs are low-energy folk/lofi. The genre wall was stronger than the acoustic preference.

---

## Energy Continuity Across Mood Changes

**High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock:**
Both want 0.9 energy. High-Energy Pop got Sunrise City. Deep Intense Rock got Storm Runner. Same energy level, completely different songs because genre is the filter.

Surprise? Storm Runner ranked #1 for rock at 69.8, but Sunrise City for pop at 98.4. The scores show that Sunrise City is objectively "better" (happy + high energy together), but Storm Runner wins the rock search because it's the only rock option and genre matters most.

---

## The Cheat Code: Being Happy + Energetic

Sunrise City, Gym Hero, and Rooftop Lights all combine "happy" mood with "high energy" (0.76-0.93). These three songs dominate because they satisfy the most common user combination. Users asking for anything else (sad music, low energy, rare genres) fall through the cracks.
